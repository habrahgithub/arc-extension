## Axis Assessment

### Strong findings accepted

1. **Messaging clarity needs improvement**
   - current public value proposition is too systems-heavy
   - concrete phrasing about risky AI-generated code at save/commit is a better wedge

2. **Open VSX parity matters**
   - Marketplace-only emphasis creates conversion and ecosystem friction

3. **ARC-specific trust pages are required**
   - reusing DocSmith/payroll-oriented security/privacy language is a credibility risk

4. **Telemetry contract should be explicit**
   - privacy-first/no-content capture is a strategic differentiator and must be stated crisply

5. **First-run demo / evaluation flow is strategically important**
   - onboarding must prove value before governance friction is felt

6. **Enterprise packaging is a distinct workstream**
   - signed artifacts, checksums, rehost/private-marketplace guidance, and procurement materials are all valid future requirements

7. **Retention and engagement metrics matter more than raw installs**
   - especially 3-day retention, save-gate engagement, override/dispute rate, and latency

### Findings accepted with constraints

1. **Progressive enforcement / coach mode**
   - useful as a retention hypothesis
   - must not silently weaken current fail-closed or WARDEN-governed protected-surface behavior

2. **Override / dispute workflow**
   - valuable for retention and regulated teams
   - requires explicit Axis/Warden policy review before implementation

3. **Pricing and monetization bands**
   - directionally reasonable as market references
   - must not be treated as product truth before retention and interview evidence exist

### Deferred findings

1. **Bank / tier-1 enterprise pursuit**
   - valid future path, but not current rollout priority
   - requires ARC-specific trust pack, enterprise distribution posture, and validated retention

2. **Authority / backend expansion**
   - any Vercel/Railway authority packaging remains deferred to future review

3. **Remote metrics/upload assumptions**
   - telemetry must remain local-first and opt-in unless separately reviewed
